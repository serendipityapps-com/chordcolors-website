# Organic content — 10 concepts + 2-week calendar (no face, no filming skill needed)

Everything below is a screen recording of the app + text captions. Record on iPhone
(Do Not Disturb ON), trim in the TikTok/CapCut editor, add auto-captions. Post natively to
TikTok, then re-export WITHOUT the TikTok watermark (save your originals!) and cross-post to
Instagram Reels + YouTube Shorts — three surfaces, one recording. Always use the app's own audio.

**Account setup:** handle @chordcolors, bio: "See why music works 🎨🎹🎸 Free on iOS ↓" + App
Store link. Reply to every comment in week 1 — the algorithm feeds on early conversation, and
questions become next videos.

## The 10 concepts (hooks written for the first line of text on screen)

1. **"Why do these 4 chords sound SO good?"** — play I–V–vi–IV, colors pulse, caption reveals:
   "the colors are neighbors. that's the answer." (This is the flagship — remake variants of it.)
2. **"Name the song from its colors"** — show a famous progression's color sequence, let it play,
   answer at the end. Comment bait: "drop your guesses." Recurring series.
3. **"This is (kinda) how synesthetes see music"** — play something emotional, colors reacting.
4. **"Every key is a color wheel"** — circle of fifths reveal, one key lighting up at a time.
5. **"Major vs minor — SEE the difference"** — same root, two chords, two color moods.
6. **"What makes a tritone sound evil?"** — play it, show the colors are opposites. "Clashing
   colors = clashing notes."
7. **"The chord that makes every song sadder"** — borrowed iv chord, colors shift cold.
   (Ties into your existing borrowed-chords blog post — link it in comments.)
8. **"148 scales exist. Here's the Japanese one."** — hirajoshi playing, exotic color pattern.
   Recurring series: one culture's scale per video.
9. **"POV: music theory finally clicks"** — quick cuts: confused-face emoji caption → colors
   playing → "oh." Meme format, lowest effort, remake weekly.
10. **"I made this app because sheet music made me quit piano"** — founder story over app
    footage. Personal + indie-dev angle; these routinely outperform product demos.
