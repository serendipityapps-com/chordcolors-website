# Chord Colors — Creator Brief

*(Send this after a creator says yes. Also paste into Billo / TikTok Creative Challenge if you use them.)*

**What it is:** A free iOS app that turns music theory into color. Chords and scales are colored
by the circle of fifths — similar colors sound good together, clashing colors sound tense. You can
*see* why a progression works. Try it in-browser (no download): https://chordcolors.com
App Store: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/chord-colors-piano-and-guitar/id1422598441

**Audience:** Beginner/intermediate piano & guitar players, songwriters, self-taught musicians
who find theory intimidating.

**Goal:** Make a viewer stop scrolling and think "that just made music theory click — I want to
try it." Success = App Store downloads.

**Creative freedom:** Format, angle, tone, and hook are yours — you know your audience better
than I do. Angles that might spark ideas (use or ignore):
- "Why do these 4 chords sound SO good?" (play I–V–vi–IV, let the colors answer)
- "This is (kind of) how synesthetes see music"
- "I never understood keys until I saw them"
- React/duet a famous progression, then show its colors
- "Day X of learning — this finally made chords make sense" (journey accounts)

**Only 3 must-haves:**
1. The app's colors reacting on screen, **with sound**, inside the first ~3 seconds.
2. Say or show that it's **free on the App Store** (name: "Chord Colors").
3. Use the app's own audio for the music — it's cleared for ads. No copyrighted songs
   (this matters because I'll run the video as an ad).

**Requirement:** You play piano or guitar — any level from confident-beginner up is fine
(the audience is learners, not virtuosos).

**Don't:** call it a game, or promise it teaches piano/guitar in a week.
